Fernandinho and Oscar in the Champions League

17th Dec 2014

As a counterpoint to the last post, it was interesting now that the group stages are over to look at Fernandinho and Oscar's Champions League performances

Their CL usage is very similar - both playing roughly 60 minutes a game in 5 appearances. While Oscar's per-90 attacking stats are similar to his league ones, he is being asked to do less mid-field battling and defensive stuff than in the league. In contrast, Fernandinho is doing less offensively and considerably more defensive and mid-field tussling than in the league.

A quick comparison of oil club midfielders

28th Nov 2014

Although Chelsea appear to have been playing better than Man City through the first third of the English league season, it may not be that unreasonable to compare the Opta stats (via Squawka) their Brazilan midfielders. Perhaps the most interesting thing here is how similar Fernandinho and Oscar's non-shooting (per 90m) stats are, even down to their rates of fouling and being foulded, albeit Fernandinho has more purely defensive duties - reflected in the interceptions, blocks and clearances stats.
